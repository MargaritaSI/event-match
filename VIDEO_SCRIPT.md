# EventMatch — Video Demo Script (~2:30)

**Format:** screen recording of the live site on a phone‑sized window
(https://margaritasi.github.io/event-match/), your voice over it. **No background music.**
Speak ~10% slower than feels natural. Keep moving — don't linger on any screen.

> Tip: pre‑load the site and have a second browser/incognito window open for the share‑link moment so
> there's no waiting on camera. Cut any dead time in editing.

---

### 0:00 – 0:25 · Hook (the problem) — *talking head or title card*
> "Quick question: have you ever met someone amazing at a conference… swapped contacts… and a week
> later had no idea who they were or why you were supposed to message them?
> We forget most of the people we meet within a day — and networking is the whole reason we go.
> I built **EventMatch** to fix that."

*(On screen: app logo + tagline "Meaningful Matches for Modern Events.")*

---

### 0:25 – 0:35 · What it is
> "EventMatch is a mobile networking companion for tech events, built with **KendoReact**. It's three
> things — **Me**, **Meet**, and **Event** — and it closes the whole networking loop."

*(Show the header with the three groups; it's already live and deployed.)*

---

### 0:35 – 1:05 · Discover & match — *People tab*
> "Here are the people at the event. Say I'm into health, mobile and design — EventMatch ranks
> everyone by **shared interests *and* concrete skills**, and flags **intent matches**.
> Look — it surfaces a recruiter who's *hiring* against my *open‑to‑work*, and a designer I share
> Swift and UI/UX with. I can filter by language, design discipline or sport, or search anything."

*(Tap the filters, tap a person to expand — show the shared‑skill chips highlighted green and the
🎯 intent match. Tap "Want to meet" → the match dialog pops.)*

> "One tap to connect — and now their contacts are unlocked."

---

### 1:05 – 1:35 · Capture & follow‑up — *the core idea, Quick Capture → Tasks*
> "But meeting people isn't the hard part — **following up** is. So right after a conversation I open
> **Quick Capture**, pick the person, and note the next step…"

*(Link an attendee, type "send portfolio", pick "in 3 days".)*

> "…and EventMatch automatically creates a **dated follow‑up task**. I can export it straight to my
> Apple or Google Calendar, or get an email reminder. That's the part that turns a 'nice to meet you'
> into an actual outcome."

*(Show the task appear in Tasks; click it → the linked profile opens with contacts.)*

---

### 1:35 – 2:00 · The wow: real matching, no backend — *Share link*
> "Here's my favourite bit. There's **no server** — your card lives only on your device. So how do two
> people actually match? My Card → **share link**. It encodes my card into a URL."

*(Copy the share link, paste it into the second/incognito window.)*

> "Open it on another phone… and there's my card. Tap **Connect**… contacts unlocked. Two strangers,
> matched, with zero backend. That's the demo path two judges can try right now."

---

### 2:00 – 2:20 · Value for everyone — *fast montage*
> "And it's not just attendees. **Groups** are lasting communities with a Discord that outlives the
> event. **Sponsors** get badge‑drop lead capture and a live ROI dashboard. And **organisers** get a
> real‑time dashboard — top interests, match potential, a zone crowd heatmap — all computed live from
> the data."

*(Swipe quickly: Groups → a group's example discussions; Sponsors → "Share my badge" + leads/views;
Dashboard → the bars and zone heatmap.)*

---

### 2:20 – 2:30 · Close
> "It's live, deployed, mobile‑first, fully built in KendoReact, and backed by 55 unit tests.
> Try it yourself at **margaritasi.github.io/event‑match**. Thanks for watching!"

*(End card: live URL + GitHub link.)*
